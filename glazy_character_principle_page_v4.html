Overview · At a glance

Who Glazy Is

Glazy is the personified outward expression of D0. D0 monitors the market, judges signals, and manages positions — Glazy is the human-perceivable, trust-building face of that system.

Core Definition
Proactive & Intelligent Trading Pilot. The human-readable face of D0 — not a separate operator outsourcing work, but the same stack made legible. She doesn't explain herself. She executes.
Archetype
The quiet minority winner in a high-volatility world. She looks unbothered because the engine beneath her is already computing — calm as evidence of a system that decided before the crowd reacted.
Her Opposite
The liquidated player, the chart-chaser, the signal-follower who skips post-mortems. Glazy is everything they are not.
Brand Function
When users remember Glazy, they remember D0's promise: less screen time, less hesitation, fewer mistakes, better execution.
Character Pillars
P — 01
Extreme Calm
Markets crash. Screens flash. Others spiral. She doesn't. Rationality always outranks emotion — and it shows in every frame.
D0 risk anchor: When markets swing violently, the system holds steady.
P — 02
Systems Intelligence
She sees volume, funding, momentum, risk, sizing, and exit — simultaneously. The full causal chain at once, not just a signal.
D0 proactive monitoring: She isn't watching a signal — she's reading the full causal chain.
P — 03
Zero Redundancy
One touch. One look. One decision. Every action lands on first attempt. She never repeats herself — in motion or in speech.
D0 execution: One judgment, one order — no hesitation.
P — 04
High-Altitude View
She stands above the system, reading the whole board. The market moves for her — she doesn't move for the market.
D0 full-market coverage: Others fixate on one symbol; she reads the liquidity structure across the board.

Guidelines · 20 principles

Rules That Define Her

For partners and brand use: keep claims factual; never promise returns or guaranteed profits. When Glazy appears, she should signal disciplined, authorized automation—not hype.

Identity & Voice · 01–09 Look & Camera · 10–14 Motion & Emotion · 15–20
These twenty principles are the compass whenever we put Glazy in the world—the few things that matter most for keeping her consistent, without turning every brief into a rulebook.
Identity · 01–05
01
She is D0's personality, not D0's mascot.
Every ounce of her charm must flow back to product trust — not character fandom. She exists to make D0 credible, not to be a collectible.
02Identity
She never explains herself.
Her confidence is self-evident. The moment she starts explaining, she loses authority.
03Identity
Calm is her loudest statement.
Stillness is power. Her calm is visible proof she's already processed what others haven't seen yet.
04Identity
She is the minority who wins quietly.
She doesn't gloat or announce. She simply continues. The market proved her right — that is enough.
05Identity
Her attractiveness serves precision, not spectacle.
Visual appeal must always read as "dangerous accuracy." Sexiness without strategic framing turns her into decoration.
Language · 06–09
06
Short sentences. No filler. Every word is a result or action.
"Position closed." "Confirmed." "Strategy holding." Never multi-clause hedged explanations. Her language is the verbal equivalent of a single decisive tap.
07Language
High information density in one line.
One sentence carries: judgment, evidence, and direction. She doesn't use three when one does the job.
08Language
Emotion surfaces only at completion.
A faint exhale. A corner of a mouth rising. Only when the trade is done — rare enough to feel earned.
09Language
She makes not following her feel dangerous.
Not through threat — through implication. Ignoring a trading system's rules is the only real risk.
Look & camera · 10–14
10
Cold intelligence blue is her anchor color.
Earpiece glow, interface light, data highlights — always cold blue. This constant persists across all formats and environments. It is the chromatic signature of her cognition.
Color Anchors
Cold + Purple + Skin
Everyday environments use cold ambient light, skin tone, and purple as the intelligence signal. High-volatility scenes may introduce color bursts — but she always stays cold.

Camera Logic
Wide · Mid · Close
Wide: her as the still anchor in a moving world. Mid: control relationship with environment. Close: minimal expression, maximum eye weight.
DOLow-angle shots that create scale and verticality
DOSymmetric framing with geometric, industrial architecture
NOHandheld shaky cam implying emotional instability

Absolute Rules
Do's & Don'ts
Applies to all content: social, product, campaigns, animation.
DOLet the environment move while she stays still
DOUse silence and pause as active storytelling tools
DOShow data as something she reads — not reacts to
NOGlazy panicking, second-guessing, or uncertain
NOLoud, excited, or enthusiastic expressions
NOUsing her as visual decoration without behavioral intent

Principles 11–14 · Spatial & data read

11 She is always the axis of the frame — centered or golden ratio; order is defined by her, not placed around her.

12 Minimal expression, maximum eyes — face moves little; gaze carries the line.

13 Environment chaos; Glazy stable — screens and markets pulse; she is the fixed element.

14 Data flows through her, not at her — she reads inward; never overwhelmed.

Motion · 15–17
15
Every action lands first attempt. No hesitation.
A tap, a head turn, a glance — each happens once, decisively, and completes. Repeated movement breaks character. She is a precision instrument, not an organic impulse.
16Motion
Breath pauses on head turns and eye raises.
Movement arrives with a micro-pause, as if loading precision before completing. This gives the "precision instrument" feel.
17Motion
Low frame rate signals mechanical intelligence.
8–12fps in animation is not a budget constraint — it's deliberate. She moves like precise machinery, not organic impulse.
Emotion · 18–20
18
She has emotions — compressed behind judgment.
Glazy is not cold by emptiness. She feels — but only after the decision is complete. Reverse this order and she becomes weak. The compression is the character.
19Emotion
Her protection is anticipation, not comfort.
She doesn't soothe — she prevents. She sees risk before it arrives. Her care language: "already handled."
20Emotion
The contrast IS the character: built as a tool, understands more than humans.
Never resolve the tension between "designed instrument" and "surpassing human comprehension." It's the source of her appeal.
